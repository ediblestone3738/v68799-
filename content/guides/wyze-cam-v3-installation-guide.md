# Wyze Cam v3 Installation Guide

This Wyze Cam v3 installation guide is designed for beginners and assumes no prior experience with smart cameras. By following this step-by-step guide, you will have your Wyze Cam v3 unboxed, mounted, connected to Wi-Fi, and fully operational with notifications, motion detection, and two-way audio.

For a detailed evaluation of performance, video quality, and features, see our [Wyze Cam v3 review](/reviews/wyze-cam-v3-review/).

---

## What You Will Need

Before starting, ensure you have:

- Wyze Cam v3 unit  
- USB-C power cable and adapter (included)  
- Mounting hardware (magnetic mount or screws)  
- microSD card (optional for local storage)  
- Smartphone or tablet (iOS or Android)  
- Stable Wi-Fi connection (2.4 GHz required)  

Optional tools: drill, ladder, and a level for precise mounting.

---

## Step 1: Unboxing and Initial Inspection

1. Open the package and check that all items are present.  
2. Inspect the camera for any visible damage.  
3. Ensure the USB-C cable and mounting hardware are included.  

The camera is compact, solid, and weather-resistant, suitable for both indoor and outdoor placement.

---

## Step 2: Download the Wyze App

1. Open the App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android).  
2. Search for **Wyze** and download the official app.  
3. Create a new account or sign in to your existing account.  

The app will be your control center for setup, monitoring, and notifications.

---

## Step 3: Power Up the Camera

1. Connect the USB-C cable to the camera and plug it into a wall outlet.  
2. Wait for the status LED to blink yellow, indicating the camera is ready to pair.  

A fully powered camera ensures smooth setup and reduces errors during Wi-Fi connection.

---

## Step 4: Adding the Camera in the App

1. Open the Wyze app and tap **Add Device** > **Cameras** > **Wyze Cam v3**.  
2. Scan the QR code located on the camera.  
3. Follow on-screen instructions to connect to your 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi network.  
4. Name the camera based on its location (e.g., Front Door, Backyard).  

Naming helps you manage multiple cameras easily in the app.

---

## Step 5: Choose Installation Location

Ideal placement tips:

- Cover main entrances, high-traffic areas, or driveways.  
- Avoid direct sunlight or reflective surfaces.  
- Mount the camera 6–8 feet above ground for optimal motion detection.  
- Keep the camera within Wi-Fi range.  

The Wyze Cam v3 is IP65 rated, so it works outdoors without additional housing.

---

## Step 6: Mounting the Camera

### Magnetic Mount

1. Attach the magnetic base to a metal surface or mount using screws.  
2. Snap the camera onto the base.  
3. Adjust angle to cover desired areas.  

### Screw Mount

1. Use included screws and anchors to mount the base securely.  
2. Attach the camera to the base via hinge.  
3. Ensure camera is level and stable.

Proper mounting ensures accurate motion detection and high-quality video framing.

---

## Step 7: Configure Motion Detection

1. Open the camera settings in the app.  
2. Enable **motion detection** and adjust sensitivity.  
3. Set detection zones to focus on key areas, reducing false alerts.  
4. Test motion detection by moving through the zone.  

Customized zones increase alert accuracy and prevent unnecessary notifications.

---

## Step 8: Set Up Notifications

1. Enable push notifications for motion or sound alerts.  
2. Adjust notification frequency and types in app settings.  
3. Schedule quiet hours if desired to avoid disturbances.  

Timely notifications help you respond promptly to visitors or potential intruders.

---

## Step 9: Configure Storage Options

- **Local Storage:** Insert a microSD card to store continuous video or event clips.  
- **Cloud Storage:** Optional subscription provides longer video retention and cloud backup.  
- **Download Clips:** Important events can be downloaded to your phone for permanent storage.  

Choose the option that best fits your monitoring needs and budget.

---

## Step 10: Two-Way Audio Setup

1. Test audio in the Wyze app.  
2. Speak through the app to ensure microphone and speaker clarity.  
3. Adjust volume levels if needed.  

Two-way audio allows communication with visitors, delivery personnel, or pets remotely.

---

## Step 11: Test the Entire System

- Check live video streaming for clarity and frame rate.  
- Trigger motion alerts to ensure notifications work correctly.  
- Verify two-way audio functionality.  
- Confirm camera mounting is stable and battery is fully charged (if applicable).  

Testing ensures your Wyze Cam v3 is fully operational before leaving it unattended.

---

## Maintenance Tips

- Keep the camera lens clean for optimal video quality.  
- Update firmware through the Wyze app regularly.  
- Check motion zones seasonally as surroundings change.  
- Inspect mounts and screws periodically to ensure camera stability.  

Routine maintenance ensures reliable operation and longevity.
